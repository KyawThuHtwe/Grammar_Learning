so ... that ➫ enough ... to


  • so ... that သည် ရှေ့ဝါကျမှ နာမဝိသေသန၏ အကျိုးဆက်ကို ဖော်ပြသော ပုံစံဖြစ်သည်
  • so + adj + that ကို adj + enough ပုံစံရေးပါ
  • Subject များတူလျင် that ဝါကျမှ Subject ကိုဖြုတ်ပါ
  • Subject များမတူလျင် that ဝါကျမှ Subject ကို for ခံပြီးရေးပေးရသည်
    • I ➟ for me
    he ➟ fo him
    she ➟ for her
    we ➟ for us
    they ➟ for them
  • can/ could ကိုဖြုတ်ပြီး to နှင့်ဆက်ပါ
  • Subject မတူသော ဝါကျများကို ဆက်သွယ်ရာတွင် Verb နောက်တွင် ရှေ့ဝါကျမှ Subject ကို ညွှန်းသောစကားလုံးများ ဖြုတ်ပေးရသည်
Examples:
1. Thura was so fast that he could win the race.
Thura was fast enough to win the race.
2. Wadi is so beautiful that she can become an actress.
Wadi is beautiful enough to become an actress.
3. The stream is so shallow that we can wade across it.
The stream is shallow enough for us to wade across.
4. The novel was so short that I could read it in one hour.
The novel was short enough for me to read in one hour.
Exercises
1. These shoes are so big that they can fit your feet.

▶ These shoes are big .....

These shoes are big enough to fit your feet.

2. The room is so large that it can be used as a reception room.

▶ The room is large .....

The room is large enough to be used as a reception room.

3. He looked so handsome that he could become an actor.

▶ He looked handsome .....

He looked handsome enough to become an actor.

4. He is so foolish that he believes what they say.

▶ He is foolish .....

He is foolish enough to believe what they say.

5. The cloth is soft enough for me to use for cleaning LCD screens.

▶ The cloth is so .....

The cloth is so soft that I can use it for cleaning LCD screens.

6. The boy is clever enough to give the answer at once.

▶ The boy is so .....

The boy is so clever that he can give the answer at once.

7. Your essay is good enough to win the prize.

▶ Your essay is so .....

Your essay is so good that it can win the prize.

8. He is slim enough to slip through the fence.

▶ He is so .....

He is so slim that he can slip through the fence.
